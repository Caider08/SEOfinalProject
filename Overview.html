    Search Engines have two major functions:

    1. Crawling and Indexing

      2. Search engines use links to crawl through all the unique documents on the web(web pages, PDFs, JPGs, etc..). Once the pages are found, the search engine's crawlers/spiders decipher the code from them and store the pieces in databases inside massive datacenters all over the world. Inside these databases the pages are ranked and indexed for accessing later.

    2. Provide answers to user search queries

      3. Search engines scour its stored documents for answers and rank them according to Relevance and Popularity

    Importance of Relevance and Popularity demonstrated in stats from a 2011 study by Slingshot SEO:

    • A #1 position in Google's SERP receives 18.2% of all click-through traffic
    • The second position receives 10.1%, the third 7.2%, the fourth 4.8%, and all others under 2%.
    • A #1 position in Bing's search results averages a 9.66% click-through rate
    • The total average click-through rate for first ten results was 52.32% for Google and 26.32% for Bing
